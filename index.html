Albert Einstein

The Physicist who Changed the World

A portrait photo taken in 1947
"Everybody is a genius. But if you judge a fish by its ability to climb a tree, it will live its whole life believing that it is stupid."
Most known for his equation E=MC2, Albert Einstein's work on Special Relatviity revolutionized the world's understanding of physics.
  • 1879: Albert Einstein is born is Ulm, Wurttemberg.
  • 1885: Einstein attends a Catholic elementary school.
  • 1889: Max Talmud, a family tutor, introduces Einstein to the world of science and mathematics.
  • 1896: Einstein graduates highschool at 17 and attends the Federal Polytechnic School in Zurich.
  • 1901: Einstein acquires Swiss citizenship.
  • 1903: Einstein marries his college sweetheart, Mileva Maric.
  • 1905: Einstein and Maric's first son, Hans Albert Einstein, is born.
  • 1907:Einstein completes his Special Theory of Relativity.
  • 1909:Einstein leaves his job at the Patent Office and becomes an Associate Professor at Zurich University.
  • 1910: Einstein and Maric's second son Eduard Einstein is born.
  • 1911: Einstein is appointed Professor of Theoretical Physics at the German University of Prague.
  • 1913:Einstein is appointed Professor of Theoretical Physics at the Federal Institute of Technology in Zurich.
  • 1916: Einstein's General Theory of Relativity is published.
  • 1919: Einstein divorces his wife Maric and marries his cousin, Elsa Einstein Loewenthal.
  • 1933: Adolf Hilter is appointed Chancellor of Germany and Einstein emigrates to the United States.
  • 1940: Einstein becomes a citizen of the United States.
  • 1945: Einstein is appointed chairman of the Emergency Committe for Atomic Scientists
  • 1955: Einstein passes away.
